## MATERIAL VULNERABILITY HEAT MAP (2024)

### Risk Scoring Methodology
- **Supply Concentration**: Geographic concentration of mining/refining
- **Geopolitical Risk**: Political stability, sanctions, trade tensions
- **China Dependency**: % of material flowing through Chinese processing
- **Alternative Availability**: Ease of switching to alternative sources
- **Strategic Importance**: Criticality to battery production

**Risk Levels**:
- CATASTROPHIC (9-10): Immediate threat to >60% of battery production
- CRITICAL (7-8): Major disruption risk affecting 40-60% of production
- HIGH (5-6): Significant vulnerability affecting 20-40% of production
- MEDIUM (3-4): Moderate risk with manageable alternatives
- LOW (1-2): Minimal vulnerability, diversified supply

### Risk Score Breakdown

#### GRAPHITE - CATASTROPHIC VULNERABILITY (10/10)

**Supply Concentration**: 9/10
- China: 65% of global natural graphite mining
- Mozambique: 15% (limited processing capacity)
- Madagascar: 8% (no processing capacity)
- U.S.: <0.1% (essentially zero)

**Refining Chokepoint**: 10/10
- **95%+ of spherical graphite** processed in China
- Requires 7-10 processing steps with proprietary Chinese technology
- No Western alternative capacity at scale

**Geopolitical Risk**: 8/10
- China implemented graphite export permit system (December 2023)
- Threatened further restrictions if semiconductor export controls continue
- Taiwan tensions could trigger full embargo

**China Dependency**: 10/10
- 78% of U.S. graphite imports directly from China
- Even Mozambique-sourced graphite often processed in China before U.S. import
- ZERO U.S. domestic spherical graphite production

**Strategic Impact**:
- **If China halts graphite exports**: 70-80% of U.S. battery production stops within 6 months
- **No near-term substitute**: Silicon anodes (reduce graphite 50%) still in R&D phase
- **2030 Outlook**: Even with ALL projects online, still 50% China-dependent

**Mitigation Status**: INADEQUATE
- Syrah Vidalia (LA): Only 11,250 tonnes/yr (2.5% of 2030 U.S. demand)
- Graphite One (AK): 42,000 tonnes/yr - feasibility study only, 2029+ target
- Westwater (AL): 7,500 tonnes/yr - permitting delays
- **Total 2030 domestic capacity**: ~100,000 tonnes (22% of demand)

---

#### COBALT - CRITICAL VULNERABILITY (8/10)

**Supply Concentration**: 9/10
- Democratic Republic of Congo: 70% of global mining
- Katanga province produces 80% of DRC cobalt
- No other country can scale to replace DRC supply

**Refining Chokepoint**: 8/10
- China refines 65% of global battery-grade cobalt
- DRC ore → China refiners → Battery chemicals → U.S. plants
- U.S. domestic cobalt refining: ZERO capacity

**Geopolitical Risk**: 9/10
- **DRC Political Instability**: Armed conflicts, mining disputes, labor concerns
- **China Leverage**: Controls refining even when cobalt mined elsewhere
- **Price Volatility**: 2021-2022 spike (300% increase) during supply scares

**China Dependency**: 7/10
- 52% of U.S. cobalt imports are Chinese-refined
- 8% direct from DRC (often through Chinese-owned smelters)
- Canadian sources (Glencore) provide only 15% of U.S. imports

**Strategic Impact**:
- **DRC disruption**: 30-50% cobalt supply offline → prices spike 200-300%
- **Battery maker response**: Shift to LFP (cobalt-free) or high-nickel NMC (5% Co vs 10-15%)
- **Grid storage impact**: LFP cells (mostly Chinese) become only option

**Mitigation Status**: IMPROVING
- **Recycling Game-Changer**: 50%+ of 2030 cobalt demand met by recycling (Redwood, Li-Cycle)
- **Chemistry Shift**: LFP (cobalt-free) growing from 30% to 50% of U.S. battery market
- **Canadian Expansion**: Glencore, Electra Battery Materials refining capacity
- **2030 Outlook**: Recycling + Canadian sources reduce DRC dependency to 30-40%

#### LITHIUM - HIGH VULNERABILITY (6/10)

**Supply Concentration**: 5/10
- Australia: 47% (diversified across multiple mines)
- Chile: 26% (SQM, Albemarle duopoly in Salar de Atacama)
- China: 14% (domestic mining)
- Argentina: 6% (growing)
- U.S.: 1% (Silver Peak only operational mine)

**Refining Chokepoint**: 8/10
- **China refines 60%** of lithium hydroxide/carbonate globally
- Australian spodumene → Shipped to China → Refined to hydroxide → Exported
- U.S. refining capacity: <10% of domestic demand

**Geopolitical Risk**: 5/10
- **Chile**: Stable democracy, but new constitution debates lithium nationalization
- **Australia**: Strong ally, but 95% of mined lithium shipped to China for refining
- **China**: Could restrict lithium chemical exports (owns 60% of refining)

**China Dependency**: 7/10
- 35% of U.S. lithium imports directly from China (refined)
- 60% of global supply chain flows through Chinese refiners
- Even Australian/Chilean lithium often refined in China before U.S. delivery

**Strategic Impact**:
- **China refining halt**: 40-50% of U.S. lithium supply disrupted
- **Chile nationalization**: 5-10% price increase, minimal supply disruption
- **Alternative chemistries**: Sodium-ion (for stationary storage) reduces lithium demand 10-15%

**Mitigation Status**: STRONG PROGRESS
- **Domestic Mining Expansion**:
  - Thacker Pass (NV): 40,000 tonnes/yr (2027)
  - Rhyolite Ridge (NV): 26,000 tonnes/yr (2028)
  - Kings Mountain (NC): 30,000 tonnes/yr (2027)
  - **Total 2030**: 143,500 tonnes/yr (29% of demand)
- **Domestic Refining**:
  - Albemarle Kings Mountain: 30,000 tonnes/yr
  - ExxonMobil Arkansas: 110,000 tonnes/yr (2027)
  - Piedmont Tennessee: 30,000 tonnes/yr
  - **Total 2030**: 220,000 tonnes/yr (44% of demand)
- **Australian Partnership**: Co-investment in Kwinana, Kemerton hydroxide plants - direct shipping to U.S.
- **2030 Outlook**: 65% non-China sourcing (domestic + allied)

#### NICKEL - MEDIUM-HIGH VULNERABILITY (5/10)

**Supply Concentration**: 6/10
- Indonesia: 48% (but mostly low-grade nickel pig iron)
- Philippines: 12% (low-grade)
- Russia: 9% (SANCTIONED - eliminated from Western supply)
- Canada: 5% (but 15% of Class 1 refining - HIGH VALUE)
- Australia: 6% (Class 1 production)

**Refining Chokepoint**: 6/10
- China: 25% of Class 1 nickel, 45% of nickel sulfate (battery-grade)
- Indonesia/Philippines ore → China HPAL plants → Nickel sulfate → Battery precursors
- **Class 1 vs Class 2 Critical**: Batteries require 99.8%+ purity (Class 1)

**Geopolitical Risk**: 6/10
- **Russia Sanctions**: Eliminated 18% of Class 1 refining capacity from Western markets
- **Indonesia Resource Nationalism**: Banned nickel ore exports (2020) to force domestic processing
- **Canadian Stability**: Strong ally, but limited scalability vs. global demand

**China Dependency**: 6/10
- 22% of U.S. nickel imports directly from China (refined)
- 45% of nickel sulfate processed in China from Indonesian feedstock
- Canadian supply (35% of U.S. imports) provides strong alternative

**Strategic Impact**:
- **Russia sanctions**: Forced shift to Canadian/Australian sources (successful adaptation)
- **Indonesia export ban**: Increased prices 30-40%, but China processing absorbed supply
- **China nickel sulfate halt**: 30% of U.S. battery production affected (vs. 70% for graphite)

**Mitigation Status**: STRONG ALTERNATIVES
- **Canadian Partnership**:
  - Vale Sudbury, Glencore nickel refineries (35% of U.S. imports)
  - Electra Battery Materials refining cobalt by-product nickel
  - **BASF-Talon JV**: 75,000 tonnes/yr nickel sulfate (Tamarack mine - 2028)
- **Domestic Mining**:
  - Eagle Mine (MI): 18,000 tonnes/yr (operating)
  - Tamarack (MN): 60,000 tonnes/yr Class 1 (2028 target)
- **Recycling**:
  - 30% of 2030 nickel demand from recycled batteries (Redwood, Li-Cycle)
- **2030 Outlook**: 50-55% import dependency (down from 95% in 2024) - BEST improvement trajectory

### Stockpile Release Triggers

1. **Price Spike Trigger**: Material price increases >100% above 3-year moving average for 30+ days
2. **Supply Disruption**: Major source country (China, DRC, Chile) halts exports for geopolitical reasons
3. **Natural Disaster**: Earthquake, typhoon disrupts 20%+ of global supply (e.g., Chilean lithium region)
4. **Armed Conflict**: Taiwan Strait, South China Sea escalation affecting shipping routes

### Stockpile Replenishment Mechanism

- **Royalties on Federal Mining Leases**: 5% of lithium/cobalt/nickel production on federal lands
- **Recycling Allocation**: 10% of federally-funded recycling facility output (Redwood, Li-Cycle DOE loans)
- **Defense Production Act Purchases**: During periods of oversupply, DPA authorizes low-cost stockpile build

## CONCLUSION: MANAGING RESIDUAL RISK

### The Unavoidable Reality (2030)

**Even with ALL mitigation strategies successfully executed, U.S. battery supply chain will retain vulnerabilities**:

1. **Graphite**: 40-50% China-dependent (no alternatives at scale)
2. **Cobalt**: 20-30% DRC-dependent (no substitute for specific chemistries)
3. **Lithium**: 35% China-refined (cost advantages persist)
4. **Nickel**: 20% China-processed (marginal vulnerability)

**Total China Dependency**: Reduced from 70-80% (2024) to 35-45% (2030) - acceptable resilience level

### Risk Acceptance Framework

**ACCEPTABLE** (Managed Vulnerability):
- **35-45% China dependency** across portfolio of materials
- **Multiple sourcing alternatives** exist (even if more expensive)
- **Strategic reserves** provide 6-24 month buffer
- **Recycling** contributes 30-40% of cobalt/nickel supply

**UNACCEPTABLE** (Critical Vulnerability):
- **>60% dependency on single country** for any critical material
- **>80% dependency on adversarial country** (China, Russia)
- **Zero domestic production** capacity (current graphite situation)
- **<3 month inventory** with no strategic reserve

### Final Assessment: The Path Forward

**Success Criteria for 2030**:
- ✅ Lithium: 25% domestic, 35% allied, 12% recycled = 72% non-China
- ✅ Cobalt: 5% domestic, 30% allied, 50% recycled = 85% non-China
- ✅ Nickel: 20% domestic, 35% allied, 30% recycled = 85% non-China
- ⚠️ Graphite: 15% domestic, 25% allied, 3% recycled = 43% non-China (ACCEPT RESIDUAL RISK)
