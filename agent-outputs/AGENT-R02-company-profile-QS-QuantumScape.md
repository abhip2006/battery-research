## Company Overview

**Legal Name:** QuantumScape Corporation
**Founded:** 2010 (Stanford University spin-off)
**Headquarters:** San Jose, CA
**Public Listing:** NYSE (November 27, 2020, via SPAC merger with Kensington Capital)
**Technology:** Solid-State Lithium-Metal Battery (ceramic separator)
**Value Chain:** Cell Manufacturing (Development Stage)
**Primary Application:** EV batteries
**Production Capacity:** Not Disclosed (pilot/pre-commercial)
**Employees:** ~850
**Commercialization Stage:** Stage 2 (B-sample cell production)

**Technology Description:** QuantumScape is developing solid-state lithium-metal batteries featuring a proprietary ceramic separator that replaces liquid electrolyte, enabling lithium-metal anode. Target specs: >800 Wh/L energy density, 15-minute fast charging, enhanced safety vs. conventional li-ion.

## Risk Factors (10-K Analysis)

### Technology & Commercialization Risks

1. **Manufacturing Challenges:** Transitioning from lab-scale to GWh-scale production unproven; ceramic separator manufacturing complexity
2. **Performance Validation:** B-sample cells must perform in real-world automotive conditions over 800+ cycles
3. **Cost Competitiveness:** Must achieve <$100/kWh to compete with advanced li-ion (current costs likely >$300/kWh)
4. **Timeline Slippage:** History of delayed milestones; commercial production now estimated 2027-2028 vs. earlier 2024-2025 targets

### Business Model & Financial Risks

5. **Pre-Revenue Burn:** $478M annual loss with no revenue; dependent on capital markets access
6. **Capital Requirements:** Will need $1-2B+ additional funding to reach commercial scale
7. **Dilution Risk:** Future equity raises will dilute existing shareholders significantly
8. **Licensing Concentration:** Over-reliance on VW/PowerCo partnership; if terminated, business model fails

### Competitive & Market Risks

9. **Solid-State Competition:** Toyota, Samsung, Solid Power, others pursuing similar technology
10. **Li-ion Improvement:** Conventional batteries improving faster than expected (silicon anodes, LFP cost reduction)
11. **OEM Indifference:** Auto manufacturers may decide current li-ion sufficient for EV needs
12. **Chinese Competition:** CATL, BYD massive scale advantages if solid-state economics favor incumbents

### Operational & Governance Risks

13. **Key Person Dependency:** Founder Jagdeep Singh (CEO) critical to technology development
14. **IP Protection:** Patent portfolio strength unclear; ceramic separator may not be defensible long-term
15. **Litigation Risk:** G&A expense spike due to legal costs; potential IP disputes
16. **SPAC Overhang:** Early SPAC investors may continue selling, pressuring stock
